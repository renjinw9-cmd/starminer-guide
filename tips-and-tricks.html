1. General & UI Tips

💡 #1 — Use the blueprint save system early

Save any ship design you're happy with as a blueprint before modifying it. You WILL want to revert after a bad refit. Blueprint slots are unlimited — use them.

#2 — Middle-mouse drag to free-look: Hold middle mouse button to look around your ship without changing flight direction. Essential for spotting ore deposits and enemy blips while maintaining heading.

#3 — Double-tap R for quick alignment: This auto-levels your ship to the local horizon plane. Useful after chaotic combat or mining maneuvers.

#4 — F5 quicksaves, F9 quickloads: Use them liberally before risky operations. The game auto-saves periodically, but your own quicksave is the safety net.

#5 — Zoom the camera with scroll wheel: You can zoom way out in third-person mode to see your entire ship and surroundings. Best view for docking and combat awareness.

#6 — Pin the resource overlay: Press V (default) to toggle the resource scanner overlay. It shows ore deposits as colored blips. Don't leave it on permanently — it drains scanner power.

#7 — The mini-map settings matter: Right-click the mini-map to adjust its range. Zoom out for navigation, zoom in for mining precision.

2. Mining & Resources

#8 — Yellow/orange blips are highest value: On the scanner, color indicates ore tier. Grey < Green < Blue < Purple < Orange. Prioritize orange deposits.

#9 — Asteroid belts have better density than open space: You'll find 3-5× more deposits in belt clusters than random space. Always mine in belts.

#10 — Mining laser efficiency drops with range: Get close (under 200m) for optimal yield per second. Far-range mining wastes power for less ore.

#11 — Overheating the mining laser reduces yield by 40%: If your laser temperature gauge hits red, stop immediately. The ore you get drops dramatically while output is throttled.

#12 — Drill Nodes work while you're away: Deploy a Drill Node on a deposit, go do something else, come back to full cargo containers. Passive income is the engine of mid-game wealth.

#13 — Scan asteroids before drilling: High-value asteroids often contain multiple ore types. Scanning reveals the full composition so you don't waste Drill Nodes on low-value rocks.

#14 — Ice asteroids contain volatiles for fuel: Don't skip ice deposits — they yield hydrogen and helium-3, which are essential for reactor fuel and thruster propellant.

3. Ship Design & Building

#15 — Center of mass is everything: Check the yellow COM indicator before leaving the shipyard. Thrusters aligned with COM = stable flight. Offset thrusters = uncontrollable spin.

#16 — Build small, specialized ships: One ship that mines, hauls, and fights does all three poorly. Build dedicated miners, dedicated haulers, dedicated combat ships.

#17 — Add retro thrusters (forward-facing): Without them, stopping distance is measured in kilometers. Install at least two retro thrusters on every ship over 50 tons.

#18 — Lateral thrusters for mining: Strafing thrusters (left/right/up/down) let you orbit an asteroid while keeping lasers on target. Mandatory for efficient mining ships.

#19 — Power grid: 20% headroom minimum: Your reactor should produce at least 20% more power than your ship's max draw. Running at capacity causes brownouts when you need shields or weapons most.

#20 — Use capacitor banks for burst weapons: Weapons with high burst draw (railguns, plasma cannons) need capacitor banks to buffer the spike. Without them, firing drops your shields.

#21 — The lighter the ship, the faster it turns: Every ton of cargo or armor reduces maneuverability. A stripped-down miner with 100t cargo capacity will out-turn a 500t hauler by 3×.

4. Power & Heat Management

#22 — Heat is your real enemy: Every active module generates heat. High heat signature = detected by aliens = attacked. Run cool to run quiet.

#23 — Radiators must face open space: Radiator panels need line-of-sight to the void. Burying them behind armor or other modules makes them 90% less effective.

#24 — Thermal paste doubles radiator efficiency (unlocked mid-tech tree): Apply it between heat-generating modules and radiators for a massive cooling boost.

#25 — Turn off non-essential modules when hiding: If aliens are patrolling nearby, shut down mining lasers, refineries, and non-critical systems. Your heat signature could drop by 60%.

#26 — Heat rises faster in combat than you think: Weapons generate 3-5× their listed heat during sustained fire. The "Thermal Output" stat is per-second — multiply by fire duration.

5. Combat & Defense

#27 — Kill scouts before they call reinforcements: Small alien scouts emit a signal when they spot you. Destroy them within 15 seconds or they'll summon a patrol fleet.

#28 — Shield facing matters: Shields are directional. Rotate your ship to present fresh shield faces when one side gets low. Don't just tank everything on the front shield.

#29 — Disable, then destroy: Target enemy weapons and engines first. A disabled ship is a dead ship — it can't dodge or shoot back.

#30 — Don't fight near your station: Combat draws more enemies. If you fight next to your base, you're putting your entire infrastructure at risk. Intercept threats at least 5km out.

#31 — Heat-seeking missiles are best against fast targets: Small alien drones dodge projectile weapons easily. Missiles with thermal tracking almost always hit.

#32 — Keep one weapon slot for a point-defense turret: PD turrets automatically shoot down incoming missiles and small drones. One PD turret saves more shield HP than an extra armor plate.

6. Economy & Money

#33 — Processed resources sell for 3-5× raw ore: Never sell raw ore. Refine it first. The price multiplier alone justifies building refineries early.

#34 — Check Reclamation 9 prices before selling: Prices fluctuate based on supply and demand. Wait for a high-price window before offloading your stockpile.

#35 — Rare earth metals have the best profit margin: Neodymium, yttrium, and scandium sell for 10-20× more than iron or silicon. Prioritize rare earth deposits.

#36 — Pay off debt as late as possible: The starting debt accrues interest slowly. Investing credits in more mining ships yields higher returns than early debt repayment.

#37 — Automated haulers multiply income: A single hauler running ore from Drill Nodes to your refinery generates passive income while you explore or fight.

7. Automation & Logistics

#38 — Set up the Drill Node → Hauler → Refinery chain early: This is the most important production chain in the game. The sooner it's running autonomously, the sooner you scale.

#39 — Name your haulers by route: "Hauler-Asteroid7-Refinery" tells you exactly what it does. Generic names lead to confusion when you have 10+ ships.

#40 — Link Gates save hours of travel time: Once you unlock Link Gates, place them between your mining outposts and main station. Instant cargo transfer across star systems.

#41 — Set cargo limits on haulers: Don't let haulers wait for a full load if the route is long. Set a 70% capacity trigger so they depart more frequently.

8. Survival Mode Specific

#42 — Stealth is survival: In Survival mode, aliens are more aggressive and resources are scarcer. Keep heat signature below 30% of max at all times unless actively mining.

#43 — Never mine in one place for more than 5 minutes: Aliens track active mining signatures. Move to a new asteroid after 3-5 minutes of drilling.

#44 — Stockpile fuel before expanding: Running out of thruster fuel in Survival is a death sentence. Keep at least 500 units of propellant in reserve.

9. Hidden Mechanics & Lesser-Known Facts

#45 — Asteroids have a "richness" hidden stat: Two asteroids that look identical may yield wildly different amounts. Rich deposits have subtle visual cracks and a denser scanner signal.

#46 — NPC traders appear near Reclamation 9: If you loiter near the Reclamation 9 station for a few minutes, NPC trade ships arrive. You can buy rare modules from them that aren't in the tech tree.

#47 — Derelict stations contain free modules: Explore abandoned structures. Many contain functional modules you can salvage — free technology without research cost.

#48 — You can push asteroids with your ship: Low on fuel? Ram an asteroid to nudge it toward your station. Slow but zero fuel cost.

#49 — Solar panels work better closer to stars: Solar panel output scales with proximity to the system's star. Build solar-powered outposts in the inner system for 2-3× more power.

#50 — Night vision mode exists: Press N to toggle night vision. Extremely useful in dark asteroid fields or shadowed areas where visual mining is difficult.

#51 — Ship naming affects nothing but makes you care more: Give your ships real names. When "Fat Rat" the hauler gets ambushed, you'll actually try to save it instead of writing it off.
