⛏️ Starminer Mining Guide: Scanner, Laser & Haulers

📅 Updated: June 10, 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read 🏷️ Core Mechanic

📑 On This Page

    ⚠️ Early Access Notice

    Last verified: June 2026 Early Access build. Mining laser power values, resource prices, and scanner behaviour may change with patches. Values shown are from specific in-game builds — verify against your current game.

    Mining Is Not Just Pointing at a Rock

    Starminer mining requires four things to work together: scanner information, the right angle and range, enough power, and time for haulers to collect. If any one of these is missing, mining fails — and the game may not clearly tell you why.

    The mining sequence is:

    1. Fly to an asteroid field — debris fields and asteroid clusters are marked or visible in space
    2. Use the scanner — identify what the rock contains before firing the laser
    3. Position correctly — line up your mining laser at the right angle and within range
    4. Fire the mining laser — the rock breaks apart into collectible fragments
    5. Wait for haulers — small collector ships automatically gather fragments and return them to your storage
    6. Check your cargo — when storage is full, haulers stop collecting

    The Scanner and "Composition Unknown"

    Before you can mine a rock, you need to scan it. Point your ship at an asteroid and activate the scanner. The scanner tells you:

    • What the rock is made of — the resource types inside
    • Whether it is worth mining — some rocks are low-value and not worth the laser time

    Why Does "Composition Unknown" Not Clear?

    If you see "Composition Unknown" and it won't resolve:

    • You are too far away — move closer. Scanner range varies by module tier.
    • You are at the wrong angle — the scanner, like the mining laser, needs line-of-sight to the rock surface.
    • Your scanner is not active — check that the scanner module is powered and online.
    • Low Energy — if your power grid is struggling, scanner operation may be degraded.

    Note: A fixed scanner range value was not clearly captured in available Early Access builds. Scanner effectiveness depends on your module tier and ship power status — test your range in-game.

    The Mining Laser — Angle, Range, and Power

    The mining laser is your primary resource extraction tool. It breaks asteroids into fragments that haulers then collect.

    Why Is My Mining Laser Not Working?

    • Wrong angle — the laser needs direct line-of-sight to the rock. If you are pointing at the wrong face or there is debris between you and the target, it will not fire.
    • Out of range — move closer to the asteroid. Laser range depends on your module tier.
    • Low Energy — mining lasers draw significant power. If your grid is weak, the laser may not have enough power to operate.
    • Mining module is offline — check that your mining laser is toggled on and powered in the module panel.
    • Storage is full — if your cargo hold has no space, mining stops because there is nowhere for the resources to go.

    Mining Laser Power Consumption

    In one shown Early Access build, a T1 Mining Laser uses approximately 20 MW of power while firing. This is a significant draw on an early-game power grid. Your exact number may change with perks, module tier, or patches — check the energy panel while firing to see your actual consumption.

    If you get Low Energy while mining, you need more power generation (solar panels) or more battery capacity before you can mine effectively. See the Power, Heat & Low Energy Guide.

    Haulers — Why Aren't They Collecting?

    Haulers are small automated ships that collect ore fragments after the mining laser breaks an asteroid apart. They fly between the debris field and your cargo hold.

    Common Hauler Problems

    • You moved away too early — cargo transfer is not instant. Haulers need time to fly back and forth. If you boost away mid-collection, fragments are left behind.
    • Storage is full — check your cargo capacity. When storage hits maximum, haulers stop because there is nowhere to put resources.
    • Cargo transfer has not finished — watch for the transfer indicator. Haulers make multiple trips for a large debris field.
    • No haulers assigned — check your fleet panel to confirm hauler ships are assigned and functional.

    The key rule: after the rock breaks apart, stay still and wait. Watch your cargo level increase before moving on.

    Resource Types — What to Mine and When

    Starminer has multiple resource types with different values and uses. The following is based on official Dev Diary information and in-game observation:

    Resource Best Early Source Primary Use Sell or Keep?
    Iron Common asteroids, debris fields Construction Keep — needed for building
    Silicates Common asteroids, debris fields Repairs Keep — needed for repairs
    Cobalt Debris fields (small fragments) Research, advanced modules Sell surplus after keeping some
    Thorium C-class asteroids (large, dark, cratered) Advanced power, high-tier modules Sell surplus — high value
    Eonite Rare asteroids (higher-tier zones) End-game modules, trading Sell surplus — very high value

    Note: Resource values are estimates based on community observation. Exact credit values per unit will vary with your current build, trade location, and any active modifiers. Check the trade terminal at Reclamation 9 for current prices.

    Where to Find Specific Resources

    • Debris fields — your best early source for Iron, Silicates, and Cobalt. Scattered small fragments almost always contain Cobalt.
    • C-class asteroids — large, dark, heavily cratered rocks. Source of Thorium. You need a T2 Refinery to process Thorium.
    • Higher-tier asteroid fields — unlocked as you progress through systems via Link Gates. Rarer resources spawn here.

    FAQ

    Why does Composition Unknown not clear?

    You are probably too far away or at the wrong angle. Move closer to the asteroid and ensure your scanner has direct line-of-sight. Low Energy can also degrade scanner performance.

    Why is my mining laser not firing?

    Check four things: angle (direct line-of-sight to rock), range (move closer), power (check energy panel — mining lasers draw significant power), and module status (is it toggled on?).

    Why are haulers not collecting?

    Most likely you moved away too early. Stay near the debris until haulers finish. Also check that storage is not full and that haulers are assigned in the fleet panel.

    What should I mine first?

    Iron and Silicates first (for construction and repairs). Once you have a buffer, target Cobalt in debris fields for credits. Thorium from C-class asteroids is your mid-game moneymaker.

    How do I find C-class asteroids for Thorium?

    Look for large, dark-coloured asteroids with heavy surface cratering. They are visually distinct from smaller, lighter common asteroids.
