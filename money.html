💰 Starminer Money Guide: Selling Resources & Early Credits

📅 Updated: June 10, 2026 ⏱️ 7 min read 🏷️ Economy

📑 On This Page

    ⚠️ Early Access Notice

    Last verified: June 2026 Early Access build. Resource prices, trade values, and mission payouts may change with patches. Check the trade terminal at Reclamation 9 for current prices.

    How to Sell Resources at Reclamation 9

    The early money loop is simple but easy to ruin. The core flow:

    1. Mine a useful load — scan first so you know what you are collecting
    2. Fly to Reclamation 9 — this is the main early-game trade station
    3. Open the trade terminal — check current prices before selling
    4. Sell only the surplus — keep what you need for construction, repairs, and research
    5. Wait for cargo transfer to complete — do not leave the station mid-transfer
    6. Spend credits on the next bottleneck — usually more power, then mining upgrades

    Cargo Transfer Is Not Instant

    When you sell resources, the transfer takes time. This is a common point of confusion:

    • Do not undock mid-transfer — if you leave before the transfer completes, you may not get paid for the full amount
    • Watch the transfer indicator — the trade terminal UI shows progress
    • Large loads take longer — a full cargo hold transfers slower than a partial load

    The same applies when buying resources or modules — wait for the transfer to finish before you leave.

    What Resources to Sell vs Keep

    This is the most common economic mistake in early Starminer. Selling the wrong resources can strand you without materials for construction or repairs.

    Resource Decision Reason
    Iron DO NOT SELL (early) Required for construction. Selling Iron early means you cannot build new modules.
    Silicates DO NOT SELL (early) Required for repairs. Running out of Silicates with a damaged ship is a bad situation.
    Cobalt Sell surplus Good early-game credit source. Keep enough for research and advanced modules.
    Thorium Sell surplus High value. Your mid-game moneymaker. Requires T2 Refinery to process.
    Eonite Sell surplus Very high value. Keep some for end-game modules.

    Note: Exact credit values per unit vary with your build, trade location, and modifiers. Cobalt and Thorium provide the best risk/reward return for early-to-mid game. Check current prices at the Reclamation 9 trade terminal before selling.

    💡 Pro Tip

    Keep a buffer of at least 50–100 Iron and 30–50 Silicates in storage at all times. If you take hull damage and have no Silicates for repairs, you are in trouble. If you spend all your Iron and then unlock a new module, you will be stuck waiting.

    Missions — Credits and XP

    Campaign missions reward both credits and XP. In the early game, missions serve double duty:

    • Credits — direct payment for completing objectives
    • XP — experience points that unlock progression milestones
    • Guidance — missions teach you game systems in a structured order

    In Sandbox mode, missions may be available but are less structured. Check the mission board at stations for available contracts.

    What to Spend Early Credits On

    Early credits are scarce. Spend them in this order of priority:

    1. More power generation — solar panels or G2 Solar upgrade. Without stable power, everything else breaks.
    2. Battery capacity — buffers against power spikes when mining, scanning, or braking hard.
    3. Mining upgrades — better scanner range or faster mining laser. More efficient mining = more credits.
    4. Heat management — Thermal Dumpers or Radiators. If heat is attracting aliens, this becomes priority #2.
    5. Research Lab — only after power, mining, and heat are stable. The Research Lab drains significant power and requires colonists.

    Do not rush to buy the biggest module available. A G2 Solar panel that costs 5,000 credits (in one shown build) is worth more than a fancy weapon you cannot power.

    Starting Debt

    You begin Starminer with some debt to pay off. This is not urgent — focus on building your income loop before aggressively paying it down. A stable mining operation generates more credits than you save by scrimping on essentials.

    Treat the debt as a background expense, not a crisis. Pay it down gradually as your mining operation becomes profitable.

    FAQ

    How do I sell resources?

    Fly to Reclamation 9 (or another trade station), open the trade terminal, select the resources you want to sell, confirm the transaction, and wait for the cargo transfer to complete before leaving.

    Why did my sale not go through?

    You probably undocked before the transfer finished. Cargo transfer is not instant. Watch the progress indicator in the trade terminal UI.

    What resources should I never sell?

    Always keep a buffer of Iron (for construction) and Silicates (for repairs). Selling these early can strand you.

    How do I make money fast?

    Early game: mine Cobalt from debris fields (good value, easy to find). Mid game: Thorium from C-class asteroids (high value). Late game: Eonite from rare asteroids in higher-tier systems. Missions also provide credits and XP.

    Should I pay off my debt immediately?

    No. Build your income loop first. A stable mining operation generates more credits than you save by rushing debt repayment.
