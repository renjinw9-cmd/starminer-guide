Best Order to Read These Guides

If you are new, do not jump straight to research or fleet upgrades. Starminer punishes players who expand before they can move, mine, sell, power the grid and control heat.

Recommended reading order.

1 First Hour Guide You just started, need a safe first-hour route, or are choosing presets and modifiers.
2 Controls & Dampeners Your ship keeps drifting, spinning, overshooting or crashing into stations.

This order keeps the cluster practical. You learn the first-hour route, stop the ship from drifting, make mining reliable, turn resources into credits, stabilize the grid, then start research without crashing the station.

What Is Starminer?

A deep-space industrial mining simulator

Starminer is an open-world space simulation sandbox developed by CoolAndGoodGames, a three-person team. Launched into Steam Early Access on May 27, 2026, it puts you in command of a mining operation in deep space. The developers plan roughly a year of Early Access development before the 1.0 release.

The game features:

  • Newtonian physics flight — 6DOF movement with realistic mass and thrust mechanics
  • Deep mining system — scan, drill, haul, and refine resources across multiple star systems
  • Modular ship & station building — design everything from small mining drones to massive orbital stations
  • Full automation chains — Drill Nodes, haulers, refineries, and logistics terminals
  • Tech tree progression — research new modules, weapons, and capabilities through Research Points
  • Heat & energy management — balance power generation, thermal output, and alien threat levels
  • Campaign, Survival, and Sandbox modes — guided learning, hardcore pressure, or free experimentation

💡 Pro Tip

Start in Campaign mode. It introduces systems gradually through missions. Sandbox gives more freedom but zero guidance — save it for when you know the mechanics. Survival is for when you understand movement, power, heat and combat.

Is Starminer Worth Playing in Early Access?

Starminer is worth considering in Early Access if you want a technical space mining and ship-building sim where movement, mass, power, heat and logistics all matter. It already has a full tutorial plus Campaign, Sandbox and Survival-style play, so there is enough structure to learn the systems and enough freedom to experiment.

The tradeoff is that it is still Early Access. Balance values, UI labels, module costs, mission flow and heat behavior may change. The development team is small (three people), and they've stated publicly that Early Access will last roughly a year, so expect iteration rather than a finished 1.0 experience.

If you want polished frictionless mining, wait. If you enjoy learning deep systems, solving power and heat problems, and turning a fragile rig into a working space operation, Starminer already has the kind of hard mechanics that make good guides necessary.

FAQ

What is the best guide to read first?

Start with the First Hour Guide. It gives you the safest first route through presets, modifiers, Campaign vs Sandbox, ship vitals, mining, selling and early building decisions.

Should I start in Campaign or Sandbox?

Campaign is easier for a first run because it teaches systems through objectives. Sandbox is fine if you want freedom, but you must self-direct the same survival loop: check vitals, scan, mine, sell, add power and expand slowly.

Why is Starminer hard at the beginning?

Starminer combines Newtonian movement, ship mass, directional tools, energy, battery, heat, cargo transfer, research costs and colonists. If one system fails, several other systems can feel broken.

What should I do first?

Pick a preset and 3 modifiers, check ship vitals, bring the energy grid online, learn dampeners, scan before mining, mine a small load, wait for haulers, sell surplus and add power before overbuilding.

How do I make money?

Mine useful resources, sell surplus at Reclamation 9, keep repair and research materials, use missions for credits and XP, and spend credits on the bottleneck that blocks your next loop.

What should I research first?

Research by bottleneck. For many early runs, power, batteries, heat control, mining efficiency, mobility or colonist support matter more than fleet, hangar or nuclear tech.

Is G2 Solar worth it early?

Yes, if power is blocking progress and you can afford it. In one shown Early Access build, Energy Solar (G2) costs 5,000 credits and requires 0 RP, making it a strong credit-based power target. Your exact cost may differ — verify in-game.

Is Starminer worth it in Early Access?

It depends on what you want. Starminer fits if you enjoy complex space engineering, physics-heavy movement and logistics problems. It may frustrate if you want a finished, polished, low-friction mining game. The current EA build has enough content to learn the systems and plenty of room to experiment.
