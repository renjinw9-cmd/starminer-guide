🚀 Starminer First Hour Guide: Getting Started

📅 Updated: June 10, 2026 ⏱️ 8 min read 🏷️ New Player

📑 On This Page

    ⚠️ Early Access Notice

    Last verified: June 2026 Early Access build. Presets, modifiers, costs, and balance values can change during Early Access. Always verify numbers against your current in-game build before planning around them.

    What Should I Do First in Starminer?

    Your first hour should be about building a stable loop, not rushing a giant station. The core loop is:

    1. Pick a preset and modifiers — understand what you're signing up for
    2. Check ship vitals — learn to read the left-side status panel
    3. Bring the energy grid online — without power, nothing works
    4. Learn dampeners — stop drifting before you start mining
    5. Scan, mine, haul — get resources flowing
    6. Sell surplus — turn ore into credits
    7. Expand slowly — add power before adding modules

    Starminer punishes players who expand before the basics work. If you can't move, mine, sell, and power your ship, adding more modules makes everything worse.

    Campaign, Sandbox, and Survival — Which Mode First?

    Starminer currently has three main ways to play. Here is what each mode is for and which one to pick first:

    Mode What It Is For Best First Step
    Campaign Guided learning through missions, systems checks and early mechanics. Follow the tutorial until your energy grid, movement, scanner, mining and selling loop are working.
    Sandbox Custom starts with more freedom and less guidance. Check vitals first, then build the same scan → mine → sell → power loop yourself.
    Survival Enemy pressure and endurance — once you understand the systems. Learn power, heat signature, controls and weapons before treating Survival as your main run.

    For most players, Campaign is the safest first run. If you jump into Sandbox, copy the same basic route manually: check vitals, scan, mine, sell surplus, add power and expand only after the ship is stable.

    💡 Pro Tip

    The developers (CoolAndGoodGames, a 3-person team) designed Campaign specifically as an on-ramp. Use it. Survival mode is tuned for players who already understand movement, power, heat and combat — jumping in early will be frustrating.

    Presets and Modifiers — What to Pick

    When starting a new game, Starminer lets you choose a preset and up to 3 modifiers. These shape your early experience significantly.

    Presets determine your starting ship layout, initial resources, and early objectives. The available presets vary between Campaign and Sandbox modes. In Campaign, presets are more structured; in Sandbox, you get more customisation.

    Modifiers are optional toggles that change game rules — things like resource abundance, enemy aggression, or starting conditions. You can pick up to 3.

    Note: The exact list of presets and modifiers may change between patches. Check the in-game menu for your current options. For your first run, the default preset with no modifiers is the safest choice — it gives you the baseline experience the tutorial was designed for.

    Reading Your Ship Vitals

    Starminer starts making sense once you read the left-side vitals panel as your real objective list. The key indicators are:

    • Credits — your money. You start with some debt to pay off.
    • XP — experience points. Earned by completing missions and objectives.
    • Energy — current power output vs consumption. If consumption exceeds output, you get Low Energy.
    • Battery — stored power reserve. Buffers against consumption spikes.
    • Storage — cargo capacity. When full, haulers stop collecting.
    • Water — life support resource. Required for colonists.
    • Heat — thermal signature. High heat attracts alien attention.

    These seven numbers are your dashboard. Before adding any module, check how it affects these vitals. A new mining laser that drains more power than you generate will break your loop.

    Safe First-Hour Route

    Here is a reliable first-hour route that works in Campaign mode:

    1. Complete the Tutorial (0–15 min)

    The tutorial walks you through basic flight, scanning, mining, and returning to base. Pay attention to dampener controls (Space to brake) — this is the #1 thing new players miss.

    2. Stabilise Your Power Grid (15–25 min)

    After the tutorial, check your energy panel. If consumption is close to or above output, add more solar panels before doing anything else. Low Energy makes dampeners weak, mining impossible, and construction slow. A single solar panel may not be enough once you start adding modules.

    3. Run the Mining Loop (25–45 min)

    Do this sequence at least 2–3 times:

    • Fly to an asteroid field
    • Scan first — identify what the rock contains before firing the laser
    • Position at the right angle and range for your mining laser
    • Mine until your storage fills or power drops
    • Wait for haulers to finish collecting — do not fly away mid-transfer
    • Return to Reclamation 9 to sell

    4. Sell Surplus, Keep Essentials (45–55 min)

    At Reclamation 9, sell what you don't need. Keep a buffer of Iron and Silicates — you need Iron for construction and Silicates for repairs. Cobalt and rarer ores are better for early credits.

    5. Expand One Module at a Time (55+ min)

    Add power first, then one functional module (another solar panel, a battery, or a scanner upgrade). Check vitals after each addition. If Low Energy appears, stop expanding and fix power.

    First Mistakes to Avoid

    • Building the Research Lab too early — it drains significant power and requires colonists. Build your mining-and-selling loop first, stabilise power, then start research.
    • Flying away before haulers finish — cargo transfer is not instant. If you move too soon, resources are left behind.
    • Selling all your Iron and Silicates — you need these for construction and repairs. Sell Cobalt, Thorium, and Eonite surplus instead.
    • Adding modules without checking power — every module draws power. Check the energy panel before and after each addition.
    • Ignoring heat signature — high heat attracts aliens. If you're not ready for combat, manage your thermal output.
    • Building too big too fast — a larger ship is harder to move, harder to power, and harder to cool. Start small.
    • Not using dampeners — Space brakes your ship. Without dampeners, Newtonian physics means you keep drifting forever.

    FAQ

    Should I build the Research Lab right away?

    No. A Research Lab without power, heat control, colonists and materials can make your station worse. Build the mining and selling loop first, stabilise power, then add research.

    How do I know if my power is stable?

    Watch the energy panel on the left. If "Low Energy" appears when you mine, brake, or scan, your grid is not stable. Add more solar or batteries before adding more modules.

    What resources should I mine first?

    In the early game, Iron and Silicates are essential for construction and repairs. Cobalt sells well for early credits. Use your scanner to check asteroid composition before mining — don't waste laser time on low-value rocks.

    How do I pay off my starting debt?

    Mine Cobalt and other mid-value ores, sell at Reclamation 9, and complete early Campaign missions which reward credits and XP.

    Can I play with friends?

    No. Starminer is single-player only. The developers have mentioned multiplayer as a possibility during Early Access, but there is no confirmed release date.
