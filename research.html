🔬 Starminer Research Guide: Early Tech Tree & RP Strategy

📅 Updated: June 10, 2026 ⏱️ 7 min read 🏷️ Progression

📑 On This Page

    ⚠️ Early Access Notice

    Last verified: June 2026 Early Access build. Research costs, RP requirements, module unlock tiers, and colonist mechanics may change with patches. Verify costs in-game before planning research paths.

    Research Is Not the First Goal

    A Research Lab without power, heat control, colonists and materials can make your station worse. The lab draws significant continuous power — adding it before your grid is stable will crash your station with Low Energy.

    Build the mining and selling loop first, stabilise power, then start research. The correct order is:

    1. Mining → selling loop working
    2. Power generation and batteries stable
    3. Heat under control (Thermal Dumpers/Radiators in place)
    4. Then build the Research Lab

    When to Build the Research Lab

    The Research Lab is a building module that generates Research Points (RP) over time. It requires:

    • Power — the lab draws continuous power. If your grid is already strained, adding the lab triggers Low Energy.
    • Colonists — you need to assign colonists to the research role. Without assigned colonists, the lab produces no RP.
    • Construction materials — the lab costs resources to build. Check the in-game build menu for current material requirements (these may change between patches).

    Note: Research Lab build costs (materials, credits, colonist count) are balance values that can change during Early Access. Check the in-game build menu for exact current costs. Do not plan your early economy around a specific number seen in an older guide or video.

    💡 Pro Tip

    Before committing to the Research Lab, check your energy panel. If your current consumption is close to your output, add another solar panel or battery first. The lab's power drain is continuous — it is not a spike you can buffer through.

    How Research Points Work

    Research Points (RP) accumulate over time while your Research Lab is powered and staffed with colonists. You spend RP to unlock new modules, upgrades, and capabilities in the tech tree.

    Key points about RP:

    • RP generation is passive — you do not "do" research. You build the lab, assign colonists, and RP accumulates over time.
    • RP generation stops if the lab is unpowered — Low Energy = no RP. This is another reason to stabilise power first.
    • RP generation stops if colonists are unassigned — colonists reassigned to other tasks stop contributing to research.
    • Some unlocks cost credits, not RP — for example, in one shown build, G2 Solar (Energy Solar) costs 5,000 credits and 0 RP. Credit-based unlocks let you progress without waiting for RP.

    What Should I Research First?

    Research by bottleneck, not by tier. The highest-tier unlock is rarely the best choice. Ask yourself what is currently blocking progress:

    Your Bottleneck Research Target Why
    Low Energy constantly Power upgrades, batteries Without stable power, nothing else works. G2 Solar (credit unlock) may be available before RP-based power upgrades.
    High heat, alien attacks Thermal Dumpers, Radiators If heat is attracting aliens, cooling research becomes the priority.
    Mining is too slow Mining efficiency, scanner upgrades Faster mining = faster credits. But only if your power grid can handle the upgraded module's draw.
    Ship handles poorly Mobility, thruster upgrades Better thrusters or dampener upgrades can fix handling issues — but check that the root cause is not Low Energy or asymmetrical placement.
    Need more colonists Colonist support, life support upgrades More colonists = more RP, more operational capacity. But they also consume more water and life support.

    What to Delay

    For most early runs, these research paths are lower priority:

    • Fleet and hangar upgrades — you do not need a fleet before you have a stable single ship
    • Nuclear power — expensive, generates massive heat, and requires advanced materials. Solar scales well enough for early-to-mid game
    • High-tier weapons — if you manage heat well, you may not need heavy weapons early. Invest in cooling and evasion before firepower
    • Station expansion modules — a bigger station needs more power, more cooling, and more colonists. Expand incrementally

    Colonists and Research

    Colonists are the workforce that operates your station. They are required for:

    • Research Lab operation — colonists assigned to research generate RP
    • Other station functions — colonists may be needed for refining, construction, and other modules

    Colonists consume water (life support). Adding colonists increases your water consumption. If you run out of water, colonists die and all station functions stop. Check your water reserves before adding more colonists.

    Note: Exact colonist counts required per module and water consumption rates are balance values that can change during Early Access. Check the in-game module and colonist panels for current numbers.

    FAQ

    When should I build the Research Lab?

    After your mining-and-selling loop is stable, your power grid has headroom, and you have heat under control. For most players, this is 1–3 hours into a Campaign run, depending on how quickly you stabilise the basics.

    How do Research Points work?

    RP accumulates passively while your Research Lab is powered and staffed with colonists. You spend RP to unlock modules in the tech tree. Some unlocks also cost credits.

    Why is my Research Lab not producing RP?

    Check three things: is the lab powered? (Low Energy = no RP). Are colonists assigned to research? (unassigned colonists = no RP). Are your colonists alive? (no water = dead colonists = no RP).

    What should I research first?

    Fix your current bottleneck. For many players: power and batteries first, then heat control, then mining efficiency. Fleet, hangar, and nuclear tech can wait.

    Is G2 Solar really 5,000 credits with 0 RP?

    In one shown Early Access build, yes. Your exact cost may differ. Check the tech tree in your current build. Credit-based unlocks are valuable because they do not consume RP — you can save RP for unlocks that require it.

    How many colonists do I need?

    Enough to staff your Research Lab and other operational modules. The exact number depends on your module count and station size. Check the colonist assignment panel in-game — it shows how many are needed for each role.
